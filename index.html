Hello!

I'm Aaruni Arora, a final-year undergraduate pursuing an integrated Master’s in Biomedical Engineering at Imperial College London. My passion lies in building creative, interdisciplinary solutions—especially for challenges in healthcare, accessibility, and education.

Over the past five years, I’ve led and contributed to impact-driven projects that combine hardware, software, and design thinking: from automating gene editing and cancer detection, to developing AR-based therapy tools for children with Cerebral Palsy. I’ve designed medical devices in OrCAD and SolidWorks, built robotics systems with MATLAB and OpenMV, and created gamified platforms in Unity.

My year in Boston included research at VIC (Harvard Medical School) and the Lab for Translational Engineering (MIT), where I worked on smart pills, Type 1 Diabetes devices, and EEG signal analysis—merging technical depth with real-world clinical needs.

Beyond engineering, I’ve explored cross-disciplinary work across health economics, investing, and hospitality. Whether it’s working on funding applications for addressing violence against women with Dr Ferrari at LSE or writing financial reports with the StockHub Analysis Group or serving in hospitality with Stint, UK, I love collaborating across fields to learn and apply new perspectives.

I'm currently open to opportunities in startups, research labs, or innovation-focused roles where I can blend technical skill with purpose-driven impact.
