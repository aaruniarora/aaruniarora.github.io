Hello!


I'm Aaruni Arora, currently in my final year of an integrated master’s in Biomedical Engineering at Imperial College London, with a Year in Industry. I am passionate about developing innovative solutions for neurodegenerative disorders such as Cerebral Palsy, Autism, and Parkinson’s Disease.

During my five-year program (2020–2025), I spent a year in Boston working at the VIC, Harvard Medical School and the Traverso Lab, MIT. My projects there involved translational research on Type 1 Diabetes, smart pills, and EEG signal analysis, providing me with invaluable hands-on experience. These projects not only allowed me to grow technically and personally but also strengthened my drive to create impactful medical technologies. At Imperial, I’ve worked on a variety of projects, from automating gene editing and cancer cell detection to designing a stethoscope in OrCAD and creating AR-based games and hydrotherapy devices for children with Cerebral Palsy. My interest in the field began in high school, when I volunteered to teach self-defense techniques to children with autism, which motivated me to make life more enjoyable and inclusive for them.

I enjoy interdisciplinary research and collaborating across fields to learn new perspectives. For instance, I am currently collaborating with Dr. Ferrari at LSE on a health economics project addressing violence against women. Beyond academia, I’ve contributed to financial research reports through the StockHub Analysis Group and worked in hospitality with Stint, UK.

Looking ahead, I’m excited to pursue a PhD in 2025, with the goal of advancing bioengineering and neuroscience to make a real-world impact.
